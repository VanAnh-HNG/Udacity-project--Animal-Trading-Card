Cat

Cats have powerful night vision, allowing them to see at light levels six times lower than what a human needs in order to see

  • Lifespan: 2-16 years
  • Daily sleep: 12-16 hours
  • Gestation period: 58-67 days
  • Mass: 3.6-4.5 kg

The cat (Felis catus) is a domestic species of small carnivorous mammal. It is the only domesticated species in the family Felidae and is often referred to as the domestic cat to distinguish it from the wild members of the family. Cats were first domesticated in the Near East around 7500 BC. It was long thought that cat domestication was initiated in Ancient Egypt, as since around 3100 BC veneration was given to cats in ancient Egypt.
